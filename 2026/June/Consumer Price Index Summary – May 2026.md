### **[^2]ABSTRACT**

May 2026 CPI shows continued inflation persistence driven primarily by energy, with headline CPI at 4.2% YoY and strong gains in gasoline and fuel prices near 40% YoY. Core inflation remains lower at 2.9% YoY but is supported by sticky services, shelter, and food prices rather than broad disinflation. Price action is uneven: energy-linked categories and transportation remain elevated, while several consumer goods categories show mild contraction or stagnation. Commodities ex-energy are largely flat, indicating limited underlying goods inflation outside energy shocks. Overall, inflation remains concentrated rather than broad-based, with energy volatility keeping headline pressure elevated and core inflation sticky enough to limit policy flexibility in the near term.

###### ***Composition***

Gasoline remained the largest inflator, increasing 8.6% MoM and 40.5% YoY. Motor fuel and energy commodities followed, surging 8% MoM and 40% YoY. Transportation prices rose 2.3% MoM and 9.3% YoY, while nondurables gained 1.3% MoM and 8.0% YoY. Electricity had a slight increase of 1.0% MoM. Used cars and trucks rose 1.5% MoM, though prices remained below previous levels. Commodities continued to be robust, rising 1.0% MoM and 5.5% YoY.
