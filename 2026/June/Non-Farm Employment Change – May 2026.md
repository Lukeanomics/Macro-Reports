# Non-Farm Employment Change – May 2026

[^1]Luke Mikho

6/3/2026

### **ABSTRACT**

Abstract The May 2026 employment data indicates a stable and resilient labor market, with nonfarm payrolls expanding by 172,000 jobs—virtually matching April’s strong, upwardly revised gains. The household survey reflects a steady labor supply, with the unemployment rate holding flat at 4.3% and the participation rate remaining unchanged at 61.8%. Headline growth was sustained by a significant shift in sector dynamics: a cooldown in private sector hiring (+120,000) was successfully counterbalanced by a sharp spike in government payrolls (+52,000), driven primarily by local government. Within the private sector, growth was highly concentrated in Leisure and Hospitality (+70,000) and Healthcare (+40,000), which offset ongoing contractions in Financial Activities (-22,000) and flatlining retail trends. Ultimately, the report depicts a mature labor market where robust public sector and service-driven hiring continue to anchor steady aggregate expansion.
