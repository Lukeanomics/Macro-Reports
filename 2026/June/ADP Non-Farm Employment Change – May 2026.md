### **ABSTRACT**

This analysis reviews May 2026 U.S. private-sector employment data from the ADP National Employment Report. Total private employment increased by 124,000 jobs month-over-month, indicating continued labor market expansion relative to April’s 109,000 gain. However, the composition of job creation suggests uneven underlying momentum across firm sizes and industries. Employment gains were concentrated in small firms, which accounted for roughly half of total job creation, while medium-sized firms showed improvement and large firms remained relatively stable. Sectorally, growth was primarily driven by the services sector, while goods-producing industries exhibited slower momentum, particularly in natural resources, construction, and manufacturing. Overall, the data indicates a labor market that remains expansionary but increasingly service-driven and uneven across cyclical segments, with signs of moderation in traditionally capital-intensive industries and more resilient contributions from smaller firms and service-oriented employment categories.

### **[^3]Interpretation**

	Latest private employment data shows continued job growth, but underlying data suggests the labor market is expanding at a moderate pace while losing its earlier cyclical strength. Total Private Employment rose 124,000 MoM, an increase of 15,000 from April’s 96,000-job gain. On paper, this reflects steady expansion over decay. However, the data shows uneven gains across sectors & firms, with growth concentrated in smaller firms and services.   
	With an addition of 67,000 jobs, small firms contributed to roughly 54% of total job gains. Suggesting the hiring strength shown is concentrated in the more flexible and low capital businesses; which typically respond quickly to short-term demand conditions. While the significant growth small firms represent indicate resilience, it also represents insufficiencies. Small firm hiring is more volatile and less durable than medium and large firms. This is due to their limited financial resources and susceptibility to economic changes. With 17,000 jobs gained Medium-sized Firms showed notable acceleration along with smaller firms, adding 2,000 jobs MoM. Providing a constructive signal, as medium-sized firms provide more stable and scalable employment growth. Large firms added 40,000 jobs in May, a decline of 2,000 compared to April’s 42,000-job gain. Suggesting the large firm hiring appetite is stabilizing over accelerating.  
Sectorally, goods slowed adding 8,000 jobs in May versus 15,000 jobs in April. While weakness was apparent in the sector, Natural Resources & Mining contributed to the majority of losses. With a decline of 3,000 jobs in May, 6,000 less than April’s gain of 3,000.  
With an addition of 114,000 jobs in May, Services remained the primary driver of employment growth with an increase of 18,000 jobs from April’s gain of 96,000 jobs. Gains were led by trade, transportation, and utilities, while education and health services remained the largest contributor regardless of slowed growth. Professional and business services reversed from weakness into solid gains in May,  signaling heightened demand for the industry. Offsetting this strength, information and financial activities remained weak, with continual pressure within rate sensitive sectors.
